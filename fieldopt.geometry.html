fieldopt.geometry.geometry.quadratic_surf_rotation(x, y, t, C)[source]

Construct an orientation vector on a quadratic surface defined by coefficients \(C\)

For some mesh-based surface, define a least squares quadratic surface parameterization \(S\):

\(f(x,y) = a + bx + cy + dxy + ex^2 + fy^2\)

\((x,y)\) are therefore parameteric coordinates that are projected to the surface \(S\). Following projection a local orientation is defined by interpolating the principal directions \((u,v)\) by \(t\).

Parameters

  • x (float) – \(x\) coordinate

  • y (float) – \(y\) coordinate

  • t (float) – Interpolation amount between principal directions \((u,v)\). Period of \(T=90\) is used for interpolation.

  • C (ndarray) – (6,) surface coefficients

Returns

  • (3,) :math:` (x,y,z)

  • (3,) normal vector
